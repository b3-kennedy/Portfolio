The Role of a Gameplay Programmer

What is a Gameplay Programmer

Gameplay Programmers write the code needed for the interactions that make the game fun to play. They work with level designers to come to an agreement on what needs to be done to make the levels and gameplay fun and engaging. Gameplay programmers write the rules that decide what objects do in the game and also work on optimisation and fixing bugs. According to payscale, the gender breakdown for the role is 88.2% male and 11.8% female.

The Role at Different Companies

Rare

At Rare, the company behind Sea of Thieves the gameplay programmer role is described as a role in which you will be working alongside other programmers, designers, testers and production staff to build innovative and exciting game features. At Rare a gameplay programmer can be expected to work on end-to-end user experience, system design, networked gameplay, artificial intelligence or user interfaces.

Required Skills and Experience:

  • Solid grounding in computer science theory and C++ development
  • At least a year's experience in the field
  • Bring enthusiasm to one area of technical expertise
  • Be enthusiastic about modern development techniques
  • A good foundation in C++ software design, programming and debugging

Ubisoft

At Ubisoft, the company behind many games, but most famously Assassins Creed the role includes ensuring the playability of the game. Responsibilities of the role include:

  • Analysing and understanding game design documents in order to define the required functinalities and the game systems that must be developed in the engine
  • Analyse existing functionalities in the engine and determine if they meet project requirements. If necessary, define how to adapt them in order to better meet game requirements
  • Collaborate with game designers/level designers/animators so as to discuss the constraints and establish the technical possibilities of the various gameplay functions
  • Seek out and correct the gameplay bugs identified by the production and quality control teams
  • Document the work so as to transfer knowledge and enable programmers and people from other areas to understand how the new systems and gameplay functions work

According to glassdoor the salary for gameplay programmer job at ubisoft is £31,000 a year, other companies like rocksteady have salaries ranging from £39,000 to £47,000
